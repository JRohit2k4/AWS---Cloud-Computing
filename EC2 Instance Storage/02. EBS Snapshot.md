**EBS Snapshot**

**What is an EBS snapshot?**
- An EBS Snapshot is a backup of an EBS volume
- It captures the state of the volume at a point in time
- Stored independently from the EC2 instance

Even if:
- The EC2 instance is terminated
- The EBS volume is deleted

You can restore the data from the data from the snapshot

**Where Do Snapshots Live?**

- Snapshots are regional
- Created in one AWS Region
- Can be used to:
  - Restore EBS volumes in different AZs
  - Be copied to other regions
 
---

**Moving EBS Data Across AZs (Very Common Exam Flow)**

1. EBS volume in ```us-east-1a```
2. Create a snapshot
3. Restore snapshot ‚Üí new EBS volume in ```us-east-1b```
4. Attach to EC2 in us-east-1b

‚ùå You cannot directly move an EBS volume across AZs  
‚úÖ Snapshot is the bridge

**2. Recycle Bin for EBS Snapshots**
- Protects against accidental deletion
- Deleted snapshots go to Recycle Bin
- Retention: 1 day to 1 year
